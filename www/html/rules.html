Background Information and Objectives

  • The game is played by two players (an Attacker and a Defender) using a board of 7 by 7 squares.
  • Each player uses 24 pieces of different colors representing their soldiers. The pieces are also called 'Faris' or 'Kalb' in Tunisian language.
  • The objective of each player is to terminate the opponent soldiers.
  • Players decide which one is the Attacker and which one is the Defender.
  • The Attacker has the advantage of starting setting his first two soldiers on the board in strategic squares to enable capturing the Defender soldiers after the first move.
  • The Defender has the advantage of requesting soldier exchanges for unreachable safe soldiers: one Defender soldier for two Attacker soldiers (one for two).
  • There are two phases for the game:
    1. a Setting phase where the players takes turns placing their soldiers on the board two at a time and
    2. a Moving phase where each player attempts to capture the opponent soldiers or request exchanges for safe/untouchable soldiers.

Setting soldiers on the board

The following rules are followed when setting the board:

  • See Figure 2.1. At this point, the board is empty.
  • The central square of the board, marked by a cross, is left empty in the setting phase. This square is used for the first move. It is called 'Malha' in Tunisian language meaning it is not seed-able by soldiers.
  • The Attacker has the advantage of starting setting his first two soldiers on the board. The Attacker's strategy to setup the opponent soldiers for capture and prevent the defender from building up safe untouchable soldiers.
  • The Defender follows the Attacker setting their two places following a strategy to capture the Attacker pieces after making captures or to build up zones of safety where unreachable untouchable pieces could demand one for two exchanges.
  • Players take turns setting the board two soldiers at a time until the board is filled up. See Figure 2.2.
  • This phase is very important in the game. Players must follow a strategy to set the opponent soldiers for capture and set their own soldiers for safety.

Figure 2.2 illustrates the end of the setting phase where the board is set with all players soldiers.

Moving soldiers on the board after setting - Rules

After completing the setting of the board, players take turn moving soldiers on the board following the following rules:

  • Soldiers could be used for moving only if they have a free/empty adjacent cell
  • Moves on the board could be orthogonal in any possible direction (up, down, left, or up) from one cell to another adjacent empty cell
  • Moves could not be made diagonally or off the board
  • The first move is always made by the Attacker who must set at least one soldier adjacent to the middle cell (Malha).
  • See Figure 3.1. At this point, all board cells are filled up with each player 24 soldiers strategically placed except the middle cell (Malha)
  • The first move of the Attacker may or may not capture one or more of the defender's soldiers. See capturing rules in the next tab
  • If the first Attacker move (with or without capture) results in the Defender being blocked, the Defender wins the game. The Defender must be allowed to move after the first Attacker move. See Figure 3.4 for an example of this case.
  • After the first move, if the move results in a player being blocked, the same player must continue moving until the opponent player is unblocked.

Capturing Soldiers

The following rules are followed:

  • A solider is captured if its gets sandwiched (placed between two opponent soldiers ) on the move (not during setting)
  • Soldiers are only captured when the opponent makes a move that sandwiches the piece
  • Soldiers are considered safe in these cases: the soldiers moves to a cell between two of the opponent's soldiers or it the soldiers is between two of the opponent's soldiers in the original setting of the board
  • A capture move move results in the capture of up to three soldiers
  • The captured piece(s) is/are removed off the board.
  • The player that made the capture is required to continue moving the same soldier to a next adjacent square if a capture is possible until no further capture moves could be made (using the same initial soldier). See Figures 4.3.
  • If no capture results from the move, players change turns.

The strategy of the game is not only to capture the opponent soldiers, but also to prevent your own soldiers from being captured. So, sometimes it may be more beneficial to not capture a and move soldiers away to places to gain ground or to prevent being captured. See Figures 4.1 and 4.2 for examples.

Exchanging Soldiers two for one

Exchanging soldiers is demanded by the Defender who managed to build up protected regions in the board where soldiers could move freely without any risk of capture. These soldiers are called untouchables and demand two of the Attackers soldiers for one untouchable. Below is the procedure/steps for making an exchange:

  1. The Defender initiates the request by raising the exchange request flag (checking the Exchange request check box) and moving a safe unreachable soldier to use for the exchange.
  2. The Attacker could accept or deny the request in his next move by raising or not raising the Exchange Request Accepted flag.
  3. The Attacker accepts the request by raising the Exchange Request Accepted flag and moving a free soldier to use in the exchange. This would be the first soldier to use in the exchange
  4. The Defender accepts this soldier by continuing to raise the exchange request flag (checking the Exchange request check box) and moving the same safe soldier to an adjacent square. If a different soldier is used in the move. The process goes back to the first step.
  5. The Attacker could accept or deny the request in his next move by raising or not raising the Exchange Request Accepted flag and make a move to indicate his second soldier to use for the exchange.
  6. The Defender accepts this second soldier by continuing to raise the exchange request flag and moving the same soldier to an adjacent soldier. With this step, the exchange request is completed and the exchanged soldiers are removed off the board.
  7. After the exchange is completed, it is the Attacker's turn to make the next move.

Game Over

Below are the possible cases when the game is over and a winner is declared

  • The Defender is blocked from moving after the first move by the Attacker: Defender wins.
  • All the Defender pieces are captured or exchanged - Attacker wins.
  • All the Attacker pieces are captured or exchanged - Defender wins.
  • The Defender resigns - Attacker wins
  • The Attacker resigns - Defender wins
